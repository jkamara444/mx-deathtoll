The Statistical Reality of Political Violence

Deaths Over Time

Political killings in Mexico
Source: Statista, 2024

The data shows an escalation in violence against journalists, activists, and political figures since 2006. Sources say this corresponds with Mexico's intensifying drug war and increasing political polarization. The years 2020 and 2022 represent the deadliest period on record. It is important to note that many different sources have varying numbers as a result of categorical differences.

Geographic Distribution & Impunity

Mexico's impunity rate of 89% for journalist murders places it among the highest globally. This means that for every 100 journalists killed, roughly 11 cases result in conviction, creating a cycle where violence becomes an effective tool for silencing opposition.
Source: Statista, 2024

Violence concentrates heavily in states with significant organized crime activity and political corruption. Veracruz, Guerrero, and Tamaulipas account for 40% of all documented cases, while Mexico City represents the highest risk for journalists specifically because of political pressure.

Challenging Normalization

Resistance to normalization requires conscious effort to reframe these deaths as what they are: political assassinations designed to preserve power. This involves:

Consistently connecting individual deaths to broader patterns

Refusing to accept "isolated incident" narratives and showing how each killing fits into systematic campaign against activists and journalists.

Humanizing victims through detailed storytelling

Sharing personal stories, backgrounds, and the human cost of each death to counter statistical numbness and maintain public attention.

Holding media accountable for problematic framing

Challenging language that treats violence as inevitable or criminal, and demanding accurate context about political motivations behind each attack.

Maintaining collective outrage and memory

Creating sustained public pressure that prevents normalization and demands justice for each victim.

Building solidarity across affected communities

Organizing cross-community support and protection networks that demonstrate violence will not break the spirit of those seeking truth and justice.

The normalization of death is one of the most insidious aspect of Mexico's crisis. When society accepts killings as routine, when outrage becomes temporary, and when each death is forgotten within a few days, violence silences both individuals, and the collective conscience.
